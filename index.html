Before and After Dinner - What to drink?

Over the last decade, craft cocktails lists and the demand for curated spirits have grown exponentially, with many restaurants offering robust drink lists deisgned to span the duration of a meal. Unforunately, little consideration is given to the traditional practice of separating the type and quantity of alochol for pre and post dinner consumption. Doing so can increase appetite, aid digestion and ideally result in less painful hangovers. Enter: Aparétifs and Digestifs.

The Aparétif

Apéritif is a French word derived from the Latin verb aperire, which means "to open". An Aparetif is an alcoholic beverage usually served before a meal to stimulate the appetite, and is therefore typically dry rather than sweet.

The Digestif

While dry and bitter help to stimulate appetite, sweet flavor-profiles with a higher alochol content can help to aid digestion. Digestifs encompass a broad category of diverse beverages, including fortified wines, brandies, herbal liqueurs and amaros.
